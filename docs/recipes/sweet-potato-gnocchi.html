Sweet potato gnocchi

Gnocchi

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • plain flour
  • 1-2 tsp salt

Roast the sweet potatoes (230 degrees celsius) for around 90 minutes.

Allow to cool and place in the fridge overnight (cover or wrap in foil).

Remove the skins and mash until smooth. Add flour gradually and mix until a loose dough is formed - just strong enough to hold its shape when you roll a handful into snakes, but will break if you lift up one end.

Cover the bench with flour, then roll all the dough into snakes (about 2cm thick) and cut into 2-3cm lengths. Optionally, you can roll these into balls and/or press with a fork to create grooves.

You can coat the gnocchi lightly with flour and put them into a bag in a single layer and freeze them. To cook, place in rapidly boiling salted water and remove when the gnocchi float to the surface.

Creamy tomato and chilli sauce

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2-3 cloves garlic chopped
  • 2 tsp chilli flakes
  • 400g diced tomatoes
  • 2-3 tbsp tomato paste (I think I used a garlic, onion and herbs one)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 200ml oat milk (less if not using cheese)
  • handful each of vegan mozzarella and cheddar

Heat oil then add the garlic and chilli. Cook for 1-2 minutes then add the diced tomatoes, then the tomato paste and oregano. Cook for a few minutes then add the oat milk, then the cheeses. Cook until thickened slightly, season to taste and then add the drained gnocchi.
